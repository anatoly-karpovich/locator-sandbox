// DOM
<button>Save</button>
<button>Save</button>
<button>Save</button>

// fragile
page.locator('button').nth(1)

// intent-driven
page.getByRole('button', { name: 'Save' }).first()
Nested text getByText 
// DOM
<p class="mut"> <strong>Welcome</strong> to our <em>platform</em> </p>

// works with normalization
page.getByText('Welcome to our platform')

// or use regex for robustness
page.getByText(/^Welcome to our platform$/)
What you’ll practice: strictness, exact vs partial, regex edge cases, filtering, has/hasText, collections, and choosing between “readable” and “stable” solutions depending on constraints.
Tip of the moment mentor mode

Prefer semantic locators (getByRole) when you want intent. Prefer structural locators when the UI is unstable, or when semantics can change frequently. Your job is to pick the compromise you can maintain.

  • Intent vs structure
  • Exact vs partial
  • Collections reasoning
  • Nested filtering
  • Readable chaining
  • Robustness trade-offs
